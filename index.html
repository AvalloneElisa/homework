Tonnarelli cacio e pepe


Step 1

Ingredients: pecorino romano cheese, black pepper, salt, 12oz tonnarelli

Details: Grate 4 oz pecorino cheese and place in a large frying pan, then grind a generous dose of black pepper over the top. Boil lightly salted water (half of what you would usually put in as the pecorino cheese is salty), and add the Tonnarelli pasta.

Step 2

Ingredients: 12oz Tonnarelli pasta, roman cheese

Details: Pour two ladles of the cooking water into the pan with the cheese and mix to obtain a creamy sauce.

Step 3

Ingredients: Tonnarelli

Details: Drain the spaghetti (al dente) and save a few ladles of the cooking water. Put the pasta in the pan with the sauce, turn the heat to very low and mix well, adding a little of the hot water put to one side as needed. Serve hot and garnish with remaining grated pecorino and a hefty pinch of black pepper.

Tonnarelli amatriciana

Step 1

Ingredients: Olive oil, guanciale, white wine, peeled tomatoes, Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper, tonnarelli pasta, grated Pecorino Romano cheese

Details: In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium-high heat until shimmering. Add guanciale and pepper flakes and cook, stirring, until lightly browned, about 5 minutes. Add wine and cook, scraping up any browned bits on bottom of pan, until nearly evaporated, about 3 minutes.

Step 2

Ingredients: 1 (28-ounce; 794g) can whole peeled tomatoes, crushed by hand, 1 pound (450g) Tonnarelli pasta

Details: Add tomatoes and bring to a simmer. Season with salt and pepper.

Step 3

Ingredients: 1 ounce (30g) grated Pecorino Romano cheese, plus more for serving

Details: Meanwhile, boil pasta in salted water until just shy of al dente, about 1 minute less than package recommends. Using tongs, transfer pasta to sauce, along with 1/4 cup pasta cooking water. Cook over high heat, stirring and tossing rapidly, until pasta is al dente and sauce has thickened and begins to coat noodles. Remove from heat, add cheese, and stir rapidly to incorporate. Season to taste with more salt and pepper. Serve right away, passing more cheese at the table.

Carciofi alla romana


Step 1

Ingredients: 8 Romanesco artichokes, Juice of 1 lemon, ¼ cup white wine

Details: Mince the garlic, parsley, and mint together. Mix in a small bowl with 1 tablespoon of the olive oil. Stuff the artichokes with the garlic mixture. Place the remaining three tablespoons of olive oil in the bottom of a tall, narrow pot. Arrange the artichokes side by side in the pot with their stems facing up. Season with salt and pepper. Cook, uncovered, over medium heat for 5 minutes.

Step 2

Ingredients: 3 cloves garlic, ¼ cup loosely-packed fresh parsley leaves, chopped

Details: Pour in the wine and enough water to reach 2 inches up the side of the pot. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid wrapped in a flat-weave dishtowel (avoiding the flame). Place a heavy nonflammable item on top of the lid to hold it in place. Simmer over medium heat, lowering the flame if the liquid begins to boil, until the artichokes are extremely tender and easily pierced with a fork, about 45 minutes. Check occasionally, and if the pot seems very dry, add water in small amounts (but keep in mind that your ultimate goal is to have very little liquid).

Step 3

Ingredients: ¼ cup loosely-packed fresh mint leaves, chopped, ¼ cup extra virgin olive oil, fine sea salt, to taste, freshly-ground black pepper, to taste

Details: When the artichokes are tender, remove the lid, turn the heat up to high, and boil until most of the liquid has evaporated and just the oil remains in the pot. (You may be able to skip this step if there is very little liquid in the pot.) Remove the artichokes with a slotted spoon, and set aside to cool, reserving the cooking liquid. When the artichokes have cooled, drizzle them with the oil from the pot. Serve this delicious spring dish warm or at room temperature.
